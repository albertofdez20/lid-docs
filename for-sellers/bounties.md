# Bounties

A bounty is an open call with a prize pool. You post the challenge, the crowd competes, winners are paid on-chain from funds already locked in escrow. Nobody has to trust that you'll pay: the money is visibly there before anyone starts working.

## How it works

1. Go to **My bounties → New bounty** in the [dashboard](https://lid.pro/dashboard).
2. Describe the challenge and the judging criteria in plain language.
3. Set the prize pool and how it splits across places (for example, three prizes of equal size).
4. Set a deadline, or leave it open.
5. Fund it. The pool plus the flat 3% fee moves into on-chain escrow at publish time.

The bounty then appears on your profile and in [Explore](https://lid.pro/discovery), with an open status, the pool size, the deadline, and a live submissions count.

## Judging and payout

Submissions arrive in your dashboard. You pick the winners. Payouts go straight from escrow to the winners' accounts, on-chain, and the bounty page shows what was paid out.

## What a bounty is good for

* **Work.** Design, content, code, translations: post the brief, pay only for the winner.
* **Growth.** Reward the community for actions that matter (create a profile, publish a product, bring a first sale).
* **Attention.** A funded pool is a credible signal in a way "DM me to collaborate" is not.

## The fine print

The pool is committed when you publish. Set judging criteria you can actually apply, and a deadline you can meet. Winners see exactly what you promised, because the chain shows it.
