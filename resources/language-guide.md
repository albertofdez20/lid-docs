# Language guide

How LID talks about itself in public. These are the lines we use and the lines we avoid.

## What to say

| Use | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Your customers are your sales team | Generic "monetization platform" framing |
| Create a product. Share a link. Get paid instantly. | Abstract taglines that require translation |
| Creators need sellers. Sellers need products. | "Creator commerce is broken" framing |
| Feels like software. Works like money. | Web3-heavy positioning language |
| Live on Solana mainnet (April 22, 2026) | "Beta" |
| Active alpha validation | "Proven traction" unless backed by metrics |
| Info-products today, agents next, physical goods after | Vague "we'll be huge" without horizon sequencing |
| Human traction today, agents tomorrow | "AI agents are already driving the business" |
| Programmable commerce routing layer | "Generic creator platform" |
| Infrastructure, not storefront | "Cheaper Gumroad" |
| Flat 3% infrastructure fee | Complicated multi-fee pricing (until tiers launch) |
| Blended ARPU of $143/year | Vague "great unit economics" |
| Completed loops, affiliate-led GMV | "We have users" (when they are invited testers) |
| Sell your products and get paid instantly | Wallet jargon, chain names, gas prompts, "connect your wallet" |
| Digital dollars | USDC (in audience-facing copy; USDC is fine in protocol and developer pages) |
| Fiat off-ramp planned post-raise | "Fiat payments available" (they are not yet) |
| Start with 3%. Pro tier comes when features justify it. | "Subscription model" (until it actually exists) |
| 100 sellers → $14K. 10K → $1.4M. 100K → $14M. | Empty TAM claims without bottom-up math |

## Tone principles

**Build, don't reverse.** Sentences move forward. We don't use "but" or "however" to pivot. Two facts side by side. The reader feels the tension without us manufacturing it.

**Show, don't tell.** If something is fast, say how fast. If something is unusual, say what's different. We never tell a reader how to feel.

**One adjective max.** Pile-ups like "powerful, innovative, seamless" empty the sentence out. Pick the one that matters or replace the adjective with a fact.

**Say what it IS, not what it isn't.** "This isn't another storefront. It's infrastructure." is lazy. "LID is infrastructure for collaborative selling." goes straight to the point.

**No hype vocabulary.** Revolutionize, leverage, seamless, unlock, elevate, cutting-edge, game-changing, paradigm shift, journey, ecosystem. Every one of these words triggers a rewrite.

**Start with the point.** No "in today's rapidly evolving landscape". No "have you ever wondered". No "we're excited to announce". The first sentence IS the hook.

**Close with action or momentum.** No recaps, no empty inspiration, no "what do you think?". The content provokes response on its own.

## What we never claim

- **Beta.** Until repeatability is earned.
- **Proven traction.** Until metrics back it.
- **AI agents are live.** Until the Agentic Commerce API ships.
- **Fiat available.** Until the off-ramp integrates.
- **Subscription tier.** Until it launches.
- **Physical goods support.** Until the Year 3+ horizon.

## What we always back with proof

- GMV claims (cumulative dollars transacted)
- Affiliate share percentages (measured from on-chain data)
- Completed loop counts (reviewed individually during alpha)
- Payout accuracy (every payout verifiable on Solana)

If we say the number, we can point at the proof.

## The ultimate test

Every piece of copy gets the same test. Would a real person write this at a bar? If it sounds like a brochure, rewrite. If it sounds like LID, ship.
