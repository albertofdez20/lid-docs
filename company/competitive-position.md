# Competitive position

LID does not compete as a cheaper Gumroad. LID operates in a different category. **Infrastructure**, not **storefront**.

## The category distinction

| Category | What they do | What they are |
|---|---|---|
| **Gumroad, Lemon Squeezy, Whop, Stan Store, Beacons** | Help creators sell digital products. | Storefronts. They help you sell. |
| **LID** | Make collaborative selling (with affiliates, instant splits, permissionless distribution) the default. | Infrastructure. We power how selling happens. |

Storefronts are destinations. Infrastructure is a layer. A storefront sells to buyers. Infrastructure connects sellers to collaborators. The business models are structurally different.

## Named competitor context

| Competitor | What they do | Where they fall short | Effective take rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gumroad** | Storefront for digital products. | No native affiliate distribution. No instant payouts. Punishes scale with high fees. | ~13% effective |
| **Lemon Squeezy** | Digital product checkout and subscriptions. | Affiliate tracking is an add-on, not native. Payouts delayed. | 5 to 10% + processing |
| **Whop** | Membership and digital product sales. | Community-focused, not distribution-focused. No permissionless affiliate infrastructure. | 3 to 10% |
| **Stan Store** | Link-in-bio commerce for creators. | Limited to individual selling. No collaborative distribution. Payout delays. | Variable |
| **Beacons** | Creator toolkit with link-in-bio, media kit, invoicing. | Broad and shallow. Not built for collaborative selling or instant settlement. | Variable |

## Why LID wins

Sell, distribute, split, and pay out in one loop.

Collaborative selling by default. Faster path from sale to earnings. Trust through smart contract execution, not reconciliation. 3% flat vs. 5 to 30% from every competitor listed above.

Every seller keeps more on LID than anywhere else.

## The architectural defense

Pricing is part of the moat. The deeper defense is structural.

Incumbents can lower fees. They cannot retrofit on-chain attribution, atomic splits, or permissionless distribution without rebuilding their stack on blockchain. That's a platform rewrite, not a pricing adjustment.

By the time a Web2 incumbent ships a competitive feature, LID's affiliate network effects will have compounded. Every completed loop on LID makes the liquidity advantage harder to catch.

## What we do differently than every competitor

**Permissionless affiliate distribution.** Every competitor gates affiliate access. LID doesn't.

**On-chain attribution.** Every competitor uses click databases. LID uses a smart contract.

**Instant payouts.** Every competitor holds money and pays on a schedule. LID settles in the same transaction.

**3% flat.** Every competitor's effective take rate is higher. Usually much higher.

**Invisible blockchain.** Every competitor in Web3 exposes wallet complexity. LID hides it.

None of these alone is the full moat. Together they describe a category that currently doesn't exist. That's the opportunity.

## What we don't do

- We don't do link-in-bio better than Beacons.
- We don't do membership gating better than Whop.
- We don't do subscription billing better than Lemon Squeezy.
- We don't try to.

LID is the rail under collaborative selling. When a creator needs a storefront primitive that LID doesn't offer, LID plays well with their existing tools. The product is the rail, not the destination.
